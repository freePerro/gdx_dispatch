# Billing & subscription

Two things share the word "billing" in your console — keep them straight:

1. **Your subscription** — what you pay us, monthly
2. **Your invoices** — what your customers pay you

Both live on the Billing page; this article covers your subscription.

## Where to find it

**Settings → Subscription.** Or click your avatar in the top right → Billing.

## What you see

- **Current plan** — Starter, Pro, or Enterprise
- **Seats** — how many users you have, vs your plan limit
- **Add-on modules** — things like Phone.com, AI Assistant, Inventory (Pro+), or Equipment Tracking (Enterprise)
- **Trial / next renewal date** — when we'll charge the card on file
- **Invoice history** — every charge from us, downloadable as PDF

## Adding a payment method

1. Click **+ Add payment method**.
2. Stripe's secure form opens.
3. Card or ACH; saved on file for the next renewal.

We never see your card number — Stripe holds it, we hold a token.

## Changing plans

- Upgrade: takes effect immediately, prorated.
- Downgrade: takes effect at next renewal. You keep current features until then.

## Adding seats

You're billed per active user. Add a seat → invite a new user → they appear on next month's bill. Deactivate a user before renewal day and they don't count.

## Pausing or cancelling

- **Pause** — keeps your data alive, no billing, read-only access. Up to 90 days.
- **Cancel** — your shop is locked after the current period ends. We hold your data for 30 days post-cancel in case you change your mind.

Both are reversible. Both are one click from this page.

## Failed payments

If a renewal fails, you'll get an email + an in-app banner. We retry the card 3 times over 7 days; if still failing, the shop goes read-only until you fix it. Read-only means nothing breaks — you just can't create new jobs, customers, or invoices.

## Tax invoices

We send a tax-compliant PDF for each charge. Your accountant can pull every invoice from Invoice history. If you need a one-off custom invoice (PO reference, different bill-to), open a support ticket.
